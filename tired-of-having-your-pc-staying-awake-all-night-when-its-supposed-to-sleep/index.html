Tired of having your PC staying awake all night when it's supposed to sleep?

Saturday, 13 January 2024

If your PC isn’t transitioning to sleep mode, it’s likely that your monitors will remain active for prolonged periods. This can be detrimental to all types of monitors, but particularly harmful for OLED displays.

If you have Steam installed on your PC, it could most likely be the cause of this issue. For some reason, Steam continues to play videos on its Store page even when the client is minimized, which keeps your GPU active and your computer awake.

Here are two potential solutions to this problem.

Diagnosing:

  • Open cmd or Powershell as administrator
    (right click start button -> Powershell/Terminal as Administrator)
  • Run powercfg /requests
PS C:\> powercfg /requests
DISPLAY:
[PROCESS] \Device\HarddiskVolume3\Program Files (x86)\Steam\bin\cef\cef.win7x64\steamwebhelper.exe
Video Wake Lock

If you find a Video Wake Lock from steamwebhelper.exe, you're affected by this issue.

There are two workarounds for this issue.

Fix 1 - Change Steam client settings

  • Right click the Steam icon, select Settings
  • Go to Interface
  • Set "Start Up Location" -> Library
  • Disable "Notify me about additions or changes to my games, new releases, and upcoming releases"
  • Restart Steam

Fix 2 - powercfg command line override

  • Open cmd or Powershell as administrator by right clicking the Start button and selecting either Windows Powershell (Admin) or Terminal (Admin)
  • Run powercfg /requestsoverride process steamwebhelper.exe display system
  • Should you ever need to revert this, run powercfg /requestsoverride process steamwebhelper.exe

 

... and a final tip

Youtube, Spotify and other sites/services will ALSO keep your PC awake!
